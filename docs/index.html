PM2.5 Sensor Readings (µg/m³)

These sensors form a chain between the Calumet industrial corridor and Hyde Park. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) is a proxy for industrial pollutants traveling through the air you are breathing, or about to breathe — elevated readings that appear first at distant sensors and then at closer ones signal an arriving plume. US EPA considers levels above 12 µg/m³ unhealthy for sensitive groups; above 35 µg/m³ unhealthy for everyone.
