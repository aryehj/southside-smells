These sensors form a chain between the Calumet industrial corridor and Hyde Park. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) is a proxy for industrial pollutants traveling through the air you are breathing, or about to breathe — elevated readings that appear first at distant sensors and then at closer ones signal an arriving plume.
