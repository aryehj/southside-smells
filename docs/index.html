Wind is currently from the northeast — a favorable direction. The Calumet industrial corridor sits 10–18 miles to the southeast of Hyde Park. When wind blows from the southeast (roughly 90°–180°), it acts as a conveyor belt carrying stack emissions and odors directly into the neighborhood. Northeast winds push those emissions away.

PM2.5 Sensor Readings (µg/m³)

These sensors measure fine particulate matter (PM2.5) — particles 2.5 µm or smaller that penetrate deep into the lungs. The sensors form a chain between the Calumet industrial corridor and Hyde Park: elevated readings that appear first at distant sensors and then at nearer ones signal an industrial plume traveling through the air you are breathing, or are about to breathe. US EPA considers levels above 12 µg/m³ unhealthy for sensitive groups; above 35 µg/m³ unhealthy for everyone.
